Our Team

 Founder -  Me. Manbodh Rana is a well distinguished terracotta artist hailing from Barpali village of north-western Odisha. Renowned for his expertise in the filed of Terracotta pottery, he has kept his family tradition of making terracotta toys and passed them into his children, he has conducted workshops to pass down terracotta techniques to young students, ensuring the preservation of this traditional artform. with focus on sculpture crafting and intricate finishing, Mr Rana plays a crucial role in upholding Barpali’s terracotta legacy. His guidance empowers aspiring artisans and his efforts contribute to the promotion of terracotta art beyond the village through exhibitions and showecases.

Premium Heritage Pottery of India

 

“MRITHIKA” is a designed and curated collection of contemporary lifestyle products in traditional terracotta crafts from heritage pottery clusters of India. Barapali, a quaint town in Odisha, India, boasts a rich terracotta craft tradition that showcases intricate patterns reflecting local beliefsand stories. Artisans skillfully etch scenes from ancient tales, religious texts, and rural life onto clay surfaces, creating a vibrant cultural tapestry. Devoted artisans and organizations are activelypreserving the craft through workshops, exhibitions, and awareness campaigns. By melding tradition with innovation, Barapali's terracotta craft can flourish, bridging the past and present for a culturallyenriched and economically empowered community.

 

We marry our design-skills with handcrafting skills of the artisans to co-create with them contemporary, functional products which are also high on aesthetics and relevance. The range includes Home décor, cookware, serve-ware and lifestyle accessories.with a vision to bring indigenous pottery crafts into the mainstream and help customers live healthy, sustainable and environment friendly lives by going back to the roots through natural and earthy terracotta ware, while also sustaining the artisans into these age-old crafts and helping preserve an integral part of India’s cultural heritage.
